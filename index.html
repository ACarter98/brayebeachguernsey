Alderney's Premier Family Beach

A sweeping sandy beach on Alderney's east coast, offering safe swimming, water sports, and views of the historic harbour breakwater.

About Braye Beach

Braye Beach showcases Alderney at its best. This 2-mile stretch of golden sand curves along the island's eastern shore, protected by one of the world's longest breakwaters. The result: calm, clear water that's perfect for families and water sports enthusiasts alike.

The beach takes its name from the adjacent harbour, itself named after the French 'braie' meaning breakwater. That breakwater - built in the 1840s and extended multiple times - creates a massive sheltered bay where the water remains flat even when the Atlantic rages outside.

Water Sports Hub

Braye is Alderney's water sports center. Kayaking, paddleboarding, and sailing are all popular here. The shelter means conditions are suitable for beginners year-round. Several local operators offer equipment hire and lessons during summer months.

Beach Amenities

Unlike Guernsey's wilder beaches, Braye offers convenience. The Braye Beach Hotel sits directly on the sand, serving food and drinks with sea views. Toilets, showers, and beach equipment hire are available. It's beach-going without the roughing it.

Visitor Tips

  • Check Alderney ferry/flight times
  • Hotel gets busy - book lunch ahead
  • Water is shallow for 50m+ at low tide
  • Breakwater walk is a must-do
  • Best swimming 2 hours either side of high tide
  • Alderney is tax-free - stock up on duty free
